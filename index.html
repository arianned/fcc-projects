A Brief History of the Brompton Bicycle

The most versatile bike there is?

The Brompton is an amazing folding bike that collapses into a tiny space - making it perfect for city living, multi-modal transport and taking with you everywhere!

    Here's a timeline of the Brompton bicycle's development:

  • 1975 - Andrew Ritchie starts designing a folding bike.
  • 1976 - The company is officially founded - named after the Brompton Oratory - a building visible from the bedroom workshop where he built the initial prototypes.
  • 1981 - Low volume, ad hoc production begins.
  • 1986 - Production begins on a larger scale - enabling the first mass-produced Bromptons to hit the market by early 1988.
  • 1988 - Full-time production starts in units set in railway arches in Brentford, West London.
  • 2002 - Will Butler-Adams joins the firm - he will become MD in 2008.
  • 2003 - A Brompton is ridden at the South Pole.
  • 2006 - The first Brompton World Championship takes place in Barcelona.
  • 2007 - New handlebar options available
  • 2008 - Brompton Wide Range Hub hub introduced
  • 2010 - Option for different gear configurations added
  • 2013 - New brake levers introduced
  • 2017 - "M" and "H" bars change appearance and new integrated brake/gear/bell units become the default.
  • 2017 - The Brompton Electric, which features technology developed with the Williams Formula One team is made available for pre-order.
